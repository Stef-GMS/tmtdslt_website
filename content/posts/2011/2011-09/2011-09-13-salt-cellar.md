Since remodeling our kitchen in the “Craftsman” or “Mission” style, I look for things that give that vintage _feel_.  
![](/images/2011/2011-09/2011-09-13-salt-cellar-img-1.jpg)

I realized that I forgot to share something with you. In the above photo, if you notice on the counter just left of the range there is a glass object. _Hubby_ bought that for me and I LOVE IT!

It’s a salt cellar… We found it at [Rejuvenation](http://www.rejuvenation.com/company/portland/index.html?iqg=6a5cd6aefbce8a741d3e421279ae31905a2ac76f) in SE Portland.  
![](/images/2011/2011-09/2011-09-13-salt-cellar-img-2.jpg)![](/images/2011/2011-09/2011-09-13-salt-cellar-img-3.jpg)

…he also bought me this cute little [porcelain spoon](http://www.crateandbarrel.com/kitchen-and-food/serving-utensils/porcelain-condiment-spoon/s669407) to go inside it from [Crate & Barrel](http://www.crateandbarrel.com/)… (Crate & Barrel have a salt cellar, but it is smaller than ours)  
![](/images/2011/2011-09/2011-09-13-salt-cellar-img-4.jpg)![](/images/2011/2011-09/2011-09-13-salt-cellar-img-5.jpg)

I don’t use a lot of salt, but it is so great having this right there by the stove and food-prep area. The spoon measures to about a 1/4 tsp. The spoon is perfect when you don’t want to stick your hands in the cellar, too. 😉
