Emily over at [The Happy Home](http://www.happyhomeblog.com/) has a cute blog. She has a multitude of posts about a variety of topics… including Home renovations and my two favorite sections [Cocktail Fridays](http://www.happyhomeblog.com/?tag=cocktail-friday) and [Recipes](http://www.happyhomeblog.com/?category_name=recipes-2).

I’m not sure how I managed to get to a post from last September, but I stumbled onto her [Banana Bread](http://www.happyhomeblog.com/?p=1214) post. She had gotten the recipe from another [blog](http://kitchenconversation.wordpress.com/2010/07/25/jacked-up-banana-bread-with-pecans/), but I didn’t care. If you have been reading my blog for a while you know I have my [Grandma B’s Banana Brea](http://TooMuchToDoSoLittleTime.com/2010/06/grandma-bs-banana-bread/)d recipe and I LOVE IT but I decided that Emily’s post couldn’t go untried. 😉

There are some key differences between Emily’s post and Grandma B’s is the following:

| **Grandma B’s** | **Emily’s post** |
| --- | --- |
| shortening | melted butter |
| sugar | brown sugar |
| 2 eggs | 1 egg   |
|  | nutmeg   |
|  | ground cloves (I only had whole so omitted)   |
|  | bourbon (like Emily, none in house, but I got some from _Ma_ and _Pa_) |

It was still piping hot and I couldn’t resist putting a little butter on it … remember… I’m [low in dietary fat](http://TooMuchToDoSoLittleTime.com/2011/03/yet-more-fat-needed/)😉  
![wpid-IMG_7263-2011-03-29-06-53.jpg](/images/2011/2011-03/2011-03-29-banana-bread-with-bourbon-img-5.jpg)

_Hubby_ said that it was good. Different from my normal banana bread… enough different that one could enjoy it and not really compare it. We could takes the alcohol flavor (oh yeah, I also used Madagascar Bourbon Vanilla which I haven’t ever used before), but not like a shot glass… the flavor was there but it was tasty! 😀

All-in-all, I will make it again! Thanks for sharing, Emily! (even if I was over 6 months late in getting to it!)
