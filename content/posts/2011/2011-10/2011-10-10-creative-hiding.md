Have you ever found a spot on your kitchen wall that you want to hide and you’re not sure what to do? We have such a spot. When we did our [remodel](http://TooMuchToDoSoLittleTime.com/category/kitchen-remodel/) we had a new phone jack put in over on the wall over by the new counter space (aka peninsula).  
![wpid-BackOfPeninsula-2011-10-10-06-40.jpg](/images/2011/2011-10/2011-10-10-creative-hiding-img-1.jpg)

Well, here’s what we were faced with looking where the phone used to “live”…  
![wpid-PhoneJack-2011-10-10-06-40.jpg](/images/2011/2011-10/2011-10-10-creative-hiding-img-2.jpg)

Not too attractive, eh? I had a tall glass jar that I used to keep pasta. But I have to admit whole wheat pasta is just not that exciting either.

Well, while we were on vacation back in August we visited the [Jacksonville Mercantile](http://www.jacksonvillemercantile.com/) in Historic Jacksonville, Oregon. They had so many cool things in their shop… the candy was wonnnnderful! 😀

But then I saw something I “had” to have. They had a multi-colored pasta that I thought would add some new color to my white kitchen and would look GREAT in my tall pasta jar. Isn’t it pretty? It’s called _[Marella Monnezzaglia Pugiese Pasta (Leftovers)](http://www.jacksonvillemercantile.com/pastifico-marella-monnezzaglia-leftovers/)_. It has all the different pasta shapes… hence the “_Leftovers_” name. 😉  
![](/images/2011/2011-10/2011-10-10-creative-hiding-img-3.jpg)

The lady at the store said that when you cook the pasta the colors don’t bleed. I haven’t used this yet… it’s sooo pretty that I don’t want to cook it. 🙁 Plus then I’d see that blasted phone jack. It was a bit spendy to buy so I hate the thought of cooking it… but I did find it on [Amazon](http://www.amazon.com/Marella-Monnezzaglia-Multicolor-Leftovers-17-6-Ounce/dp/B001XX2JFW/ref=sr_1_2?s=grocery&ie=UTF8&qid=1314955425&sr=1-2), so I need to order some from them… I really want to support the Oregon economy, so maybe I will see if they’ll price match. That’d be cool!

_So… have you done any creative hiding?_
