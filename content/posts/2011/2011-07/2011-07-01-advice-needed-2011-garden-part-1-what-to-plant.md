![](/images/2011/2011-07/2011-07-01-advice-needed-2011-garden-part-1-what-to-plant-img-1.png)As I mentioned in my _[Haven’t fallen off planet](http://TooMuchToDoSoLittleTime.com/2011/06/havent-fallen-off-planet/)_ post, I have been working on a _Garden_ post. Well, actually I have been spending the last four days recuperating. I spent the last three weekends working on my garden. Since my fall back in January, my back/lumbar/tailbone has been problematic and made it difficult to do a lot of what I like to do. It has been really difficult over the last three weekends, but I have our garden planted!! 😀 I still have to do the drip system, but the plants are planted! YIPEEEE! It’s been nice that the rain hasn’t left completely, so the garden is getting some rain.

For the previous two years I grew tomatoes and a couple of cucumber plants all in containers. Let me recap…

**Summer 2010**

This year, my friends Brent and Kate found a great deal on raised bed kits. I bought six from them for $15.00 each – retail was around $70ish each! 😀

This bed is going to be a wildflower and hummingbird/butterfly garden. I can’t wait to see it in full bloom. I used two kits for a double flower bed (8 ft x 4 ft x 8 in) and had two boards left over. (_Ma and Pa, thanks for the bricks!! 🙂_ )  
![wpid-FlowerGarden-2011-07-1-00-01.jpg](/images/2011/2011-07/2011-07-01-advice-needed-2011-garden-part-1-what-to-plant-img-4.jpg)

I combined the two leftover boards with two more kits and made this larger vegetable garden (16 ft x 4 ft x 8 in).
