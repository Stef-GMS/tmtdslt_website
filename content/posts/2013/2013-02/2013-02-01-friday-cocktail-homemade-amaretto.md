A couple of weeks ago I mentioned last week I LOVE _Disaronno Amaretto_. Downside… it can be expensive, but I’ve always thought it was worth it. Well, about a month ago, Paula from [Bellalimento.com](http://Bellalimento.com). Paula has some AMAZING recipes, one which I was compelled to make is her [Homemade Amaretto](http://www.bellalimento.com/2012/12/29/how-to-make-amaretto/). I would love to meet her and we could just sit around talking Italian food while sipping amaretto. What am I thinking… EATING Italian food and sipping on amaretto!

Anyway… I had all but one of the ingredients for her amaretto so when I was at the store yesterday I bought the missing ingredient. 😀

When I made Paula’s recipe it was too sweet for me. That sounds really weird coming from me… I have a MAJOR sweet tooth. So I had to tweak her recipe a bit. So let me share with you how I ended up making.

Have an airtight bottle or jar ready to store your amaretto. In my case, I’m using an empty _Disaronno_ bottle and a quart jar. I blacked out the “Disaronno”. 😉  
![wpid-IMG_1038-2013-02-1-08-30.jpg](/images/2013/2013-02/2013-02-01-friday-cocktail-homemade-amaretto-img-2.jpg)

Cast of Characters:  
![wpid-IMG_1026-2013-02-1-08-30.jpg](/images/2013/2013-02/2013-02-01-friday-cocktail-homemade-amaretto-img-3.jpg)  
1 1/2 Cups Water  
1 Cup Sugar  
1/2 Cup Dark Brown Sugar  
2 1/2 Cups Vodka  
1 oz Pure Almond Extract  
2 teaspoons Pure Vanilla Extract

Put the water and sugar and dark brown sugar into a heavy bottom saucepan…  
![wpid-IMG_1028-2013-02-1-08-30.jpg](/images/2013/2013-02/2013-02-01-friday-cocktail-homemade-amaretto-img-4.jpg)

…and bring it to a boil…  
![wpid-BringToABoil-2013-02-1-08-30.jpg](/images/2013/2013-02/2013-02-01-friday-cocktail-homemade-amaretto-img-5.jpg)

While the sugars are dissolving, measure out the vodka and extracts…

…2 1/2 Cups Vodka…  
![wpid-IMG_1033-2013-02-1-08-30.jpg](/images/2013/2013-02/2013-02-01-friday-cocktail-homemade-amaretto-img-6.jpg)

…2 teaspoons of Vanilla Extract… As you can see I am using my [Homemade Vanilla Extract](http://TooMuchToDoSoLittleTime.com/2012/02/homemade-vanilla-after-seven-weeks/). If you haven’t made it yet, put that on your list to do after you make this homemade Amaretto (it takes weeks to steep).  
![wpid-IMG_1034-2013-02-1-08-30.jpg](/images/2013/2013-02/2013-02-01-friday-cocktail-homemade-amaretto-img-7.jpg)

…Now for the difficult part… measure out 1 oz Pure Almond Extract… OK there really isn’t any measuring that’s necessary… the bottle is a 1 oz bottle. 😉  
![wpid-IMG_1036-2013-02-1-08-30.jpg](/images/2013/2013-02/2013-02-01-friday-cocktail-homemade-amaretto-img-8.jpg)

When the sugars have dissolved, let it cool for 15-20 minutes. I let mine go 20 minutes.

After the mixture is cool, add the vodka, vanilla and almond extract and then stir…  
![wpid-AddVodkaAndFlavors-2013-02-1-08-30.jpg](/images/2013/2013-02/2013-02-01-friday-cocktail-homemade-amaretto-img-9.jpg)

After you’re done stirring and the mixture has completely cooled off, pour it into your bottle of choice. It makes 4 Cups, so be ready. I used the old Disaronno bottle and a quart jar. Here you can see my version on the left with the “real” stuff on the right. 😉  
![wpid-IMG_1056-2013-02-1-08-30.jpg](/images/2013/2013-02/2013-02-01-friday-cocktail-homemade-amaretto-img-10.jpg)

When pouring into your bottle, be careful to not spill any of your new creation. _I wouldn’t know anything about that, would I?_  
![wpid-IMG_1053-2013-02-1-08-30.jpg](/images/2013/2013-02/2013-02-01-friday-cocktail-homemade-amaretto-img-11.jpg)

Here’s a crappy picture of the Disaronno and mine compared. Color is pretty close. Interesting since THEIRS has “carmel coloring” added. 🙁  
![wpid-IMG_1055-2013-02-1-08-30.jpg](/images/2013/2013-02/2013-02-01-friday-cocktail-homemade-amaretto-img-12.jpg)

I highly recommend you make this. It’s so super easy and less expensive and can be enjoyed as soon as you’ve made it. 😀  
![wpid-IMG_1059-2013-02-1-08-30.jpg](/images/2013/2013-02/2013-02-01-friday-cocktail-homemade-amaretto-img-13.jpg)
