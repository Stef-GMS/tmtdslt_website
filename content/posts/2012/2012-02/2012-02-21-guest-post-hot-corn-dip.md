It was an especially difficult time for me because my dad, step mom and my sister live in southwest Missouri and I’m half a continent away in Oregon. My heart ached for Dad and Sis; I felt helpless. Sis and I talked every day, she tried to sound “OK”, but I know she was just trying to be strong for Dad and her kids. When I talked with Dad he sounded so vulnerable.

Sis called on Sunday morning; my step mom had passed away. I felt sadness, but yet relief because she’d been in so much pain.

**Hot Corn Dip**  
_Recipe by Emeril Lagasse_

*   2 tablespoons butter
*   3 1/2 cups corn kernels (from 4 ears fresh white or yellow corn, or frozen fresh corn)
*   1/2 teaspoon salt
*   1/8 teaspoon freshly ground black pepper
*   1 cup finely chopped yellow onions
*   1/2 cup finely chopped red bell peppers – _I used roasted red bell peppers from a jar._
*   1/4 cup chopped green onions (green and white parts)
*   1 [jalapeno, seeded and minced](http://www.ourbestbites.com/2008/03/jalapenos.html)
*   2 teaspoons [minced garlic](http://www.ourbestbites.com/2008/03/peeling-garlic.html)
*   1/2 to 1 cup mayonnaise – _1/2 C was plenty for mine_
*   4 ounces monterey jack or cheddar, shredded- _I used monterey jack_
*   4 ounces sharp cheddar, shredded
*   1/4 teaspoon cayenne
*   Tortilla chips, for dipping

Preheat the oven to 350 degrees F.

Melt 1 tablespoon of the butter in a large heavy skillet over medium-high heat.

[![](/images/2012/2012-02/2012-02-21-guest-post-hot-corn-dip-img-1.jpg)](http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-3tcS78ToKao/T0O1D38O5iI/AAAAAAAAFCs/EQmtHa-ny3s/s1600/8808.jpg)

Add the corn, salt, and black pepper. Cook, stirring occasionally, until the kernels turn deep golden brown, about 5 minutes. Note that if you’re using _frozen_ corn it probably won’t get golden brown.  Just cook for about 5 minutes as the recipe states.

[![](/images/2012/2012-02/2012-02-21-guest-post-hot-corn-dip-img-2.jpg)](http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-V867M-EC7UE/T0O1Jqqp1UI/AAAAAAAAFC0/AOtK7eKCxgA/s1600/8810.jpg)

Transfer to a bowl.

[![](/images/2012/2012-02/2012-02-21-guest-post-hot-corn-dip-img-3.jpg)](http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-w3DAwJ8N5iA/T0O1NmTRMEI/AAAAAAAAFC8/P3wLXZOQZpY/s1600/8814.jpg)

Melt the remaining tablespoon of butter in the skillet. Add the onions and bell peppers and cook, stirring often, until the onions are wilted, about 2 minutes.

[![](/images/2012/2012-02/2012-02-21-guest-post-hot-corn-dip-img-4.jpg)](http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-qiZ6wWFSFr8/T0O1Rh8umqI/AAAAAAAAFDE/ZBWnUSFV0AI/s1600/8815.jpg)

[![](/images/2012/2012-02/2012-02-21-guest-post-hot-corn-dip-img-5.jpg)](http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-F3e3bKTSobE/T0O1R4HPucI/AAAAAAAAFDM/gE-wZyKcC1s/s1600/8816.jpg)

Add the green onions, jalapeno, and garlic and cook, stirring, for 2 minutes, or until the vegetables are softened.

[![](/images/2012/2012-02/2012-02-21-guest-post-hot-corn-dip-img-6.jpg)](http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-pnJrM8B5voo/T0O1Ya-mHQI/AAAAAAAAFDU/0wWYEvUJ2qY/s1600/8817.jpg)

[![](/images/2012/2012-02/2012-02-21-guest-post-hot-corn-dip-img-7.jpg)](http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-kVeUBejAwjc/T0O1ZK-2MYI/AAAAAAAAFDc/5HXrUCXantI/s1600/8819.jpg)

Transfer to the bowl with the corn.

[![](/images/2012/2012-02/2012-02-21-guest-post-hot-corn-dip-img-8.jpg)](http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-Yat6LgPJ4a4/T0O1dN-m2lI/AAAAAAAAFDk/uhyI6BLXakw/s1600/8820.jpg)

Add 1/2 C mayo,

[![](/images/2012/2012-02/2012-02-21-guest-post-hot-corn-dip-img-9.jpg)](http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-jYookYaTCTw/T0O1ifWQLII/AAAAAAAAFDs/EnBbg2ivLBU/s1600/8823.jpg)

cayenne pepper,

[![](/images/2012/2012-02/2012-02-21-guest-post-hot-corn-dip-img-10.jpg)](http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-iDubUaYgN3A/T0O1rAdON6I/AAAAAAAAFD8/j6zQFTNvgX4/s1600/8822.jpg)

half of the Monterrey jack and half of the cheddar

[![](/images/2012/2012-02/2012-02-21-guest-post-hot-corn-dip-img-11.jpg)](http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-mBqxCiIJ6pw/T0O1wkoik0I/AAAAAAAAFEE/_ES3MCBdoTQ/s1600/8825.jpg)

and mix well.

[![](/images/2012/2012-02/2012-02-21-guest-post-hot-corn-dip-img-12.jpg)](http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-dAlOkcY-4Yg/T0O10lObmiI/AAAAAAAAFEM/a6CB0FWHClY/s1600/8824.jpg)

If you need more mayo to hold the mixture together then add it, but I found that 1/2 C was plenty.

Pour into an 8-inch square baking dish, or something equivalent and sprinkle the remaining cheese on top. Bake until bubbly and golden brown, 10 to 12 minutes. Serve hot with tortilla chips or Fritos.

**Just so you know, this went like hotcakes!  In fact, if I make it again, I’ll have to make a double batch!
